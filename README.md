# Footstep Power Generation Using Piezoelectric Sensors

## Project Overview
This project demonstrates a **sustainable energy harvesting system** that converts **footstep pressure into electrical energy** using **piezoelectric sensors**. The generated energy is stored and regulated to power small-scale applications such as **mobile charging, street lighting, and public utility devices**.  
The system is especially suitable for **high-footfall public areas** like malls, railway stations, schools, and bus stops. 

---

## Introduction
With the rapid increase in population and electronic gadget usage, **power demand has risen significantly**. At the same time, a large amount of **kinetic energy from human movement is wasted** daily.  
This project focuses on capturing that wasted energy using **piezoelectric sensors**, which convert mechanical pressure into electrical voltage, providing an **eco-friendly and renewable power source**.

---

## Problem Statement
Traditional power generation techniques are struggling to meet growing energy demands and often rely on non-renewable resources. Additionally, energy generated from human movement in crowded public places goes unused.

This project aims to:
- Capture kinetic energy from footsteps
- Convert it into usable electrical energy
- Store and regulate power efficiently
- Provide a scalable and sustainable supplementary energy source

Key challenges addressed:
- Optimizing sensor placement and efficiency  
- Ensuring durability under continuous pressure  
- Achieving cost-effective real-world deployment

## Proposed Architecture Design
The system consists of:
- Piezoelectric sensors
- Voltage boosting circuitry
- Voltage regulation module
- Energy storage (battery)
- Microcontroller unit
- Output and alert mechanisms

---

## System Working Overview

### 1. Piezoelectric Sensors
- Capture mechanical energy from footsteps  
- Output voltage range: **3V – 4V**

### 2. Voltage Boosting & Regulation
- Voltage boosters increase output to **9V – 12V**
- Voltage regulator ensures stable output

### 3. Energy Storage & Control
- Regulated energy stored in a battery
- Microcontroller (PIC 8F4520) manages system operation
- LCD displays battery and system status

### 4. Power Applications
- Mobile charging through a **5V charging socket**
- Buzzer alerts when battery voltage drops below safe limits
- No external fuel or power source required

### 5. Target Implementation
- Schools
- Shopping complexes
- Cinemas
- Public buildings and transit areas
